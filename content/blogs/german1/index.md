The most recent approach I use to learn my german. Is just my taking videous or articles which native speakers would also consume. This articles are hard to read from the begining and even harder to understand, however it gives really interesting outcomes, because instead of being demotivated you find yourself, motivated even more. 

So I've been reading some of the journals like **SPIEGEL**. What I have red is this articles:
[This is spiegel section about professions](https://www.spiegel.de/deinspiegel/berufe-im-check-so-stellt-ein-skulpteur-puppen-modelle-und-animatronics-her-a-af4cdffe-c8f4-46f1-a04f-1db309ab56b7)
[Crash on nuerburgring](https://www.spiegel.de/sport/nuerburgring-finne-juha-miettinen-stirbt-max-verstappen-ist-geschockt-a-0f2bfba6-2f2b-4c97-837a-d030ece8b08c).
There were the other ones too but they were smaller. The other thing which I do is come back to the articles after I analysed them. The next day I just try to go through the article again, but this time faster and remembering the words I either wrote down or just translated.

Today is the first day, when I take a note in the process of studying, I think I just want to share what I do. I will be going through my course book it is `Schritte plus neu a2.1`. I'm actually now on the topic 5. I just started it.
We have a couple of new words: 
`sich schminken  - to put make up on`  
`sich anziehen - to dress`  
`ernaren - to eat`  
`sich rosiren - to shave`  
`sich beshweren - blame, complain` eg Kann ich mich nicht uber meine Nachbarn beschweren.  
We have lernt `sich` is the special partical which gives us ability to apply verb on yourself. Like `Der Vater zieht die Kinder an` and `Er zieht sich an`. This emphasizes that he dress himself.
